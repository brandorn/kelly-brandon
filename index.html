A few notes about the big day:

  • Semi-formal attire: We love an excuse to dress up, so this seems as good an occasion as any. When it comes to semi-formal attire, we’re basically saying, “Look sharp, but we’re not making you wear a tux.” Need help figuring out what to wear?

    Women: feel free to go a little fancier — wear anything from a long evening dress to a cocktail attire.

    Men: A suit, long tie, and dress shoes will fit in perfectly.

  • Parking: There is parking on the premises, right next to the venue. What luck! Here it is on a map.

  • Beach-friendly shoes: Our ceremony will be held on the beach adjacent to the venue – please either wear shoes that can make it across the sand (wedges! block heels! flats!) or bring a spare pair of flip flops.

  • Open mic: This small wedding celebration will be full of longtime friends and family — so we wanted to go a bit more nontraditional for toasts. Over dinner, anyone can take the mic to share a word of encouragement or a funny story. So, feel free to come ready to share!

4.  What to Do

Looking for places to while away the time before the wedding? Here are some of our best recommendations.

On the North Shore

Within 10-20 min of the Highland Park area.

  • Go exploring at the Chicago Botanic Garden (Glencoe)

  • Gawk at the infamous house from Home Alone (Winnetka)

  • Take in the architectural excellence of the Bahá'í House of Worship (Evanston)

  • Pay homage to Brandon’s love of watches by dropping by the The Halim Museum of Time and Glass (Evanston)

  • Observe very stressed out students at Kelly’s alma mater, Northwestern University (Evanston)

  • Catch a concert (and eat some delish pizza) at SPACE (Evanston)

In Chicago

About 45-60 min from Highland Park.

  • Do the CAC architectural boat tour. Don’t think twice. Do it.

  • Eat deep dish. (And don’t believe the haters that say Lou Malnati’s isn't the best).

  • Check out the best-in-class cultural institutions at the Museum Campus

  • See all the cute animals at the Lincoln Park Zoo (it’s free!)

  • Admire the beauty of one of Kelly’s favorite buildings in the city, the Chicago Cultural Center

  • Catch a sports ball game at Wrigley Field or Soldier Field

  • Rent a DIVVY bike and go for a leisurely ride along the Lakefront Path

  • Visit some of our fave neighborhoods! Wicker Park, Logan Square, Lincoln Park, Old Town, West Loop
